Why Blackloom

Most on-chain dashboards show you numbers. Not where they came from.

A chart is easy. Trusting the chart is the hard part. Blackloom exists because "the dashboard says X" isn't an answer on its own — you need to know what got dropped, what got deduplicated, and what's still uncertain.

Raw chain data is not clean data

Paginated APIs return duplicates. Fields go missing. Values arrive in wei, timestamps in unix epoch. None of that is usable until someone deals with it — Blackloom deals with it the same way, every single run.

A single "integrity %" hides more than it shows

Completeness, uniqueness, validity, and freshness are different failure modes. Collapsing them into one number tells you something broke, not what or how badly. Blackloom scores each separately.

Automation only counts if it's actually automatic

A pipeline you have to remember to run isn't automated, it's a chore with extra steps. Blackloom runs on its own schedule and reports its own health.

Who this is for

Anyone who needs to answer for the numbers, not just report them.

Analysts building on-chain reporting who need to show their work. Engineers who want a reference ETL pattern that handles validation and quality scoring properly instead of skipping straight to charts. Anyone learning how a real pipeline — extract, transform, load, quality, analytics — actually fits together end to end.

On-chain ETL and analytics, woven clean.
